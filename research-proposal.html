Introduction and Rationale

The Fintech industry operates in a volatile, data-rich environment where real-time insights are crucial. Dashboards are key tools, consolidating complex financial data for monitoring trends, evaluating risk, and enabling timely decisions. However, substantial gaps exist in understanding their practical effectiveness. Some dashboards fail due to poor integration, lack of interactivity, or inadequate analytical features. This research aims to investigate not just *how* dashboards are used, but *how well* they support strategic decision-making in Fintech, identifying factors contributing to success and areas for improvement.

Research Question, Aims, and Objectives

The central research question guiding this study is: How effective are dashboards in supporting decision-making within the Fintech industry, and how can their design be optimised for better outcomes?

The primary aims are:

  • To critically evaluate the effectiveness of current dashboards used for decision-making in Fintech.
  • To identify and analyse specific dashboard features (e.g., usability, customisation, data timeliness) that contribute most significantly to effective decision support.

To achieve these aims, the following objectives will be pursued:

  • Review and compare the application of different dashboard types (operational, analytical, strategic) within Fintech contexts.
  • Assess diverse user experiences, perceptions, and expectations regarding dashboards through quantitative surveys and qualitative interviews.
  • Analyse the collected data to pinpoint key usability factors, design elements, and functionalities that enhance or hinder user experience and decision quality.
  • Propose a set of evidence-based best practices or design principles aimed at improving future dashboard effectiveness in the Fintech sector.

Building on Existing Literature

This research builds upon foundational literature regarding dashboard design principles, the role of Self-Service Business Intelligence (SSBI), the importance of personalisation in wealth management dashboards, and the use of visualisation for specific tasks like fraud detection. It acknowledges the categorization of dashboards (operational, analytical, strategic) as serving distinct decision levels. However, it also incorporates counterarguments highlighting potential limitations, such as challenges with cognitive load for users with lower visualisation literacy, usability issues stemming from poor design, the accessibility barrier for non-experts due to complex metrics, and significant cybersecurity risks associated with handling sensitive financial data.

Methodology and Research Design

A mixed-methods approach will be employed to provide both breadth and depth:

  • Quantitative Phase: An online survey will be distributed to a diverse pool of Fintech dashboard users (technical and non-technical roles). Likert-scale questions will efficiently assess satisfaction levels regarding usability, customisation options, data timeliness, perceived impact on decision-making speed, and overall effectiveness. While efficient for large data sets, the limitations of Likert scales in capturing nuance are acknowledged.
  • Qualitative Phase: Semi-structured interviews will be conducted with a targeted group of participants, including Fintech professionals (e.g., product designers, compliance officers, data analysts) and potentially expert end-users. Combining survey data for broad trends with interview data for rich contextual understanding will allow for a comprehensive evaluation of dashboard effectiveness.

Ethical Considerations and Risk Assessment

Ethical conduct is paramount. The following measures will be taken:

  • Informed Consent: All participants will receive a comprehensive information sheet detailing the research purpose, data usage, storage procedures, and confidentiality measures before agreeing to participate.
  • Anonymity and Confidentiality: No personally identifiable information will be collected in surveys. Interview transcripts will be anonymised using pseudonyms, stored securely, and accessed only by the researcher.
  • Data Security: Appropriate measures will ensure secure data storage and handling throughout the research process. For sensitive discussions, offering a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) will be considered to mitigate risks associated with data sensitivity.

Key risks and mitigation strategies include:

  • Low Participant Engagement: Particularly from busy industry professionals. Mitigation involves offering flexible interview scheduling, keeping surveys concise, and clearly articulating the research value.
  • Interpretation Bias (Qualitative Data): To ensure objectivity in analysing interview data, findings and thematic frameworks will be discussed with supervisors and potentially undergo peer review or debriefing.

Formal ethics approval will be sought from the university prior to any participant recruitment or data collection.

Timeline of Activities

The proposed research will follow a structured timeline:

  • Weeks 1-3: Finalise research instruments (survey questions, interview guide), prepare ethics application materials, and submit for approval.
  • Weeks 3-5: Upon ethics approval, begin participant recruitment for both survey and interviews. Schedule interviews.
  • Weeks 5-8: Launch online survey and conduct semi-structured interviews. Monitor survey responses.
  • Weeks 8-12 (approx.): Data analysis phase. Transcribe interviews, perform thematic analysis on qualitative data, conduct statistical analysis on quantitative survey data. Synthesise findings.
  • Final 3 Weeks (approx.): Draft the final research report, incorporating analysis and findings. Revise based on feedback. Submit the final report.

This phased approach ensures adequate time for each critical stage of the research process.

Conclusion and Expected Contributions

In conclusion, while dashboards are integral to Fintech, their effectiveness is not uniform. This research will provide valuable insights into current practices, user perceptions, and critical design factors influencing decision support. By combining quantitative and qualitative data, the study aims to offer evidence-based recommendations for optimising dashboard design and implementation, ultimately contributing to more effective data-driven decision-making across the Fintech industry for both professionals and end-users.
